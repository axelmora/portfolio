📋 Special Scenarios
🚫 Run-Rule / Mercy Rule (KO)
In the WBC, a 10-run mercy rule applies after 7 innings (or 15 runs after 5 innings in pool play). If the game ends early by mercy rule, defensive outs are only those actually recorded — not padded to 27. A 10-run win in 7 innings = ~21 outs for the winner's defense.

A dominant Italy mercy-rule win gives Italy a much lower TBQ than a close 9-inning win. A dominant Mexico mercy-rule win similarly benefits Mexico's TBQ significantly vs. USA. The team that gets run-ruled records fewer defensive outs, which also affects their denominator.
⏱ Extra Innings
WBC uses the international tiebreaker rule starting in the 10th inning (runner on 2nd to start each half-inning). Every extra inning adds 3 more defensive outs per side to the TBQ denominator.

Extra-inning games tend to lower TBQ for both teams (more DO, runs are already "allocated"). The losing team in extras often surrenders a run late, slightly hurting their TBQ. If USA is watching, more extra innings can only help Mexico or Italy narrow the TBQ gap vs USA's .203.
